NASA Rodent Research in Space

Overview NASA's rodent research in space focuses on understanding the physiological and biological changes that occur in mammals during spaceflight. Rodents, particularly mice and rats, serve as valuable model organisms for studying human health, development, and disease processes, especially in the unique microgravity environment of space.

Objectives The primary objectives of rodent research include investigating the effects of microgravity on bone density, muscle mass, immune function, and overall physiological health. Researchers aim to identify the mechanisms underlying these changes and develop countermeasures to mitigate potential health risks for astronauts on long-duration missions.

NASA's rodent research in space is essential for understanding the physiological impacts of microgravity on mammalian systems. By studying these changes, scientists are better equipped to protect astronaut health during space missions and enhance our knowledge of biological processes that have implications for both space exploration and medical research on Earth.
